Middle Eastern Lentil Soup

Red lentils, onions, and carrots make up the bulk of this soup that is flavored by lemon juice, turmeric, and cumin. You can top this soup with a lemon garlic yogurt sauce for even more flavor.

CourseCuisineTime - 30 minutes
Main DishMiddle EasternPrep - 10 minutes
Cook - 20 minutes

Ingredients:

For the Soup

  • 1 medium (200 g) onion
  • 1 cup (150 g) carrots chopped
  • 1 tbsp (15 g) olive oil
  • 2 tsp (6 g) cumin
  • tsp (2 g) turmeric
  • 1 cups (350 g) red lentils
  • 2 cups (480 ml) water
  • 4 cups (960 ml) vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp (15 g) garlic
  • 3 tbsp (45 ml) lemon juice

For the Yogurt

  • cup (100 g) plain non fat Greek yogurt
  • 1 tsp (5 g) garlic minced
  • (1 ml) Splash of lemon juice
  • Water to thin
  • 1 (60 g) Roma tomato

Instructions:

For the Soup

  1. Cut the onion and carrot into a small dice. Mince the garlic.
  2. Add 1 tbsp of oil to a large stock pot and saut onion, carrot, and garlic for 3-5 minutes.
  3. Add in the turmeric and cumin. Stir.
  4. Add in the lentils, water, and broth. Bring to a boil, cover, and reduce heat to low to simmer for 15 minutes.
  5. Stir in lemon juice and season with salt and pepper to taste.

For the Yogurt

  1. Mix a splash of lemon and garlic into the yogurt. Top the soup with yogurt and tomato.
